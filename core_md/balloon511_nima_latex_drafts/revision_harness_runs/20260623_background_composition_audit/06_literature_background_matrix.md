## Source Evidence Index

- S1: XL-Calibur mission paper, https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.10608, browser lines 3-5: hard X-ray 15-80 keV balloon polarimeter, CZT focal-plane detector, anticoincidence shield, stratospheric particle background, improved shielding.
- S2: XL-Calibur mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2010.10608, PDF text lines 968-984 and 990-1023: background reduction design changes, background dominated by mainly albedo atmospheric >100 MeV neutrons and MeV atmospheric gamma rays, simulated 15-80 keV background-rate table.
- S3: XL-Calibur anticoincidence shield paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.04139, PDF text lines 31-36: BGO anticoincidence shield at ~40 km, veto threshold <100 keV, measured 20-40 keV background rate ~0.5 Hz.
- S4: XL-Calibur anticoincidence shield paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.04139, PDF text lines 151-185: stratospheric primary and secondary cosmic rays make prompt background; activation can yield delayed gamma rays outside the anticoincidence time window.
- S5: XL-Calibur anticoincidence shield paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.04139, PDF text lines 519-524 and 543-550: BGO shield passively reduces rate to ~30 Hz, active veto to ~0.5 Hz, rejects >99% charged-particle background; neutrons leak through shield.
- S6: XL-Calibur anticoincidence shield paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.04139, PDF text lines 1096-1098 and 1125-1134: flight data show 20-40 keV rate 8.2 Hz veto-off and 0.5 Hz veto-on; shield meets <1 Hz goal.
- S7: 511-CAM mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2206.14652, PDF text lines 534-540: 511-CAM scales cosmic-ray and atmospheric background measured with XL-Calibur by detector mass and expects >15 keV background rate of 60 Hz.
- S8: 511-CAM mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2206.14652, PDF text lines 551-568: MEGAlib simulations use a realistic geometry but idealized mass model; active BGO shield included to reduce background.
- S9: 511-CAM mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2206.14652, PDF text lines 583-599: simulated 511 keV peak has 390 eV FWHM, 93% reconstructed between 510.3 and 511.8 keV, single-pixel events are expected low-background, X-Calibur single-pixel selection removes >99% of background, Compton imaging can suppress events inconsistent with optics.
- S10: 511-CAM mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2206.14652, PDF text lines 612-624 and 626-632: 9 cm lens effective area 50.89 cm2 at 511 keV; dominant balloon background components modeled with Gehrels shield-leakage model; energy resolution directly affects background-limited sensitivity.
- S11: 511-CAM mission paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2206.14652, PDF text lines 626-636 and 726-729: pointed 511-CAM complements wide-field missions; point-source design has low diffuse-source detection rate, 0.17 photons/day over 0.03 photons/day background for a diffuse case.
- S12: INTEGRAL/SPI gamma-ray line spectroscopy paper, https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10139, browser lines 37-41: SPI is a space-based coded-mask Ge spectrometer with scintillation veto; cosmic rays cause large activation background; line and continuum background components vary differently.
- S13: INTEGRAL/SPI gamma-ray line spectroscopy paper, https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10139, browser lines 47-52: SPI data model includes sky signal plus large instrumental background; background is not simply subtracted.
- S14: INTEGRAL/SPI gamma-ray line spectroscopy paper, https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.10139, browser lines 63-79: SPI spectra are dominated by instrumental continuum and nuclear lines; prompt and delayed radiation processes occur; anticoincidence vetoes prompt charged-particle interactions while non-vetoed events include delayed or celestial triggers.
- S15: SPI positron-annihilation spectroscopy paper, https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.00325, browser lines 37-41: SPI 511 keV analysis uses a new instrumental background method and detailed multi-component sky fitting; reports diffuse bulge/disk line intensities.
- S16: COSI detection paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/1912.00110, PDF text lines 24-30: COSI is a balloon-borne 0.2-5 MeV wide-field high-resolution Compton telescope; 511 keV analysis separates celestial signal from instrumental background in COMPTEL Data Space.
- S17: COSI detection paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/1912.00110, PDF text lines 387-421: intense 511 keV atmospheric background line and activation lines are identified; 511 keV background has activation and atmospheric contributions; precise background description is required.
- S18: COSI detection paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/1912.00110, PDF text lines 1121-1125 and 1435-1440: full-flight background simulation includes atmospheric contribution and instrument activation; small balloon-environment variations strongly influence 511 keV backgrounds.
- S19: COSI imaging paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.10950, PDF text lines 23-32 and 37-40: 46-day balloon COSI, 0.2-5 MeV, Compton response with possible strong background suppression, strong instrumental background handled empirically, coded masks struggle with isotropic emission versus instrumental background.
- S20: COSI imaging paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.10950, PDF text lines 872-902: soft gamma-ray instrumental background includes prompt and delayed activation components; balloon 511 keV background is hard to model absolutely.
- S21: COSI imaging paper, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.10950, PDF text lines 1071-1075 and 1329-1330: COSI infers background empirically; expected instrumental background is about 100 times the 511 keV sky signal and dominates the data set.
- S22: COSI pre-flight background estimates, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25304, PDF text lines 31-43 and 54-59: COSI surveys 0.2-5 MeV with Ge detectors; MeV observations are background-dominated; cosmic-ray and albedo particles create prompt and delayed activation backgrounds; extragalactic photons dominate below 660 keV and delayed activation dominates higher energies.
- S23: COSI pre-flight background estimates, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25304, PDF text lines 130-138: COSI has high-purity Ge detectors surrounded by active BGO shields for passive attenuation and active anticoincidence.
- S24: COSI pre-flight background estimates, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25304, PDF text lines 589-643 and 857-862: continuum dominated by extragalactic photons up to 660 keV, albedo photons above; primary protons/alphas mainly contribute through activation lines; BGO veto suppresses prompt interactions, making activation lines prominent.
- S25: COSI pre-flight background estimates, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.25304, PDF text lines 1590-1603: summary repeats low-energy extragalactic dominance, higher-energy delayed activation dominance, delayed activation non-negligible especially for 511 keV.

## Cross-Instrument Sanity Judgments

1. PASS: Public literature supports the idea that prompt atmospheric/cosmic-ray background can be strongly reduced by anticoincidence and event topology in pointed balloon focal-plane instruments. Evidence: S2, S3, S5, S6, S9.
2. PASS: Public literature also supports the idea that activation/delayed internal backgrounds can be important, especially for Ge wide-field line instruments and for materials around the detector. Evidence: S12, S14, S17, S20, S22, S24, S25.
3. WARN: No cited public source justifies applying SPI or COSI absolute background fractions directly to the current TES-Laue geometry, because SPI/COSI are wide-field coded-mask/Compton instruments with different detector materials, environment, response, and background separation. Evidence: S12, S13, S16, S19, S21.
4. WARN: No cited public source justifies applying X/XL-Calibur absolute 15-80 keV or 20-40 keV rates directly to a 511 keV line-window TES-Laue final rate. Evidence: S1, S2, S3.
5. PASS: The 511-CAM literature makes the line-window caveat especially important: narrow energy resolution and single-pixel/optics-consistency selections can make final 511 keV selected background composition unlike broad-band or shield-rate composition. Evidence: S8, S9, S10.
6. PASS_WITH_CAVEAT: Public literature does not make a small final delayed fraction in a pointed, shielded, narrow-window TES-Laue 511 keV selection physically impossible. It does require that the local activity, decay sampling, veto, geometry, and source normalization audit be correct before publishing such a claim. Evidence: S4, S7, S8, S9, S10, S12, S14, S17, S20.

## X-Calibur / 511-CAM Mass-Scaling Context

PASS: The specific 511-CAM use of X/XL-Calibur background is a mass-scaled estimate of cosmic-ray and atmospheric background measured by XL-Calibur, giving an expected >15 keV rate of 60 Hz. It is not presented there as a direct delayed-activation inventory, not as a 511 keV final selected line-window fraction, and not as a proof that a different TES-Laue geometry must be delayed-dominated. Evidence: S7.

WARN: 511-CAM separately models dominant balloon background components and shield leakage for a simulated 511 keV mission, and it emphasizes energy resolution and background-limited sensitivity. Therefore a correct project comparison should distinguish mass-scaled gross background, modeled prompt/shield leakage, activation/delayed inventory, and final selected narrow-window events. Evidence: S8, S9, S10.

## What Literature Would Make Suspicious

- A close public analog would make current low delayed fraction suspicious if it were a balloon-borne, pointed, focusing 511 keV TES/Laue or channeling-optics payload with similar absorber/shield materials, similar BGO veto timing, similar narrow 511 keV window, and a flight-validated result showing unavoidable delayed/activation dominance after final selection. No such source was found in this public-literature pass. Evidence for current available analog limits: S7, S8, S11.
- Current low delayed would also look suspicious if local delayed normalization ignored the known literature risk that delayed activation can evade anticoincidence windows. Evidence: S4, S14, S20, S22, S25.
- Old delayed dominance would be physically plausible if its material inventory and selection emphasized activated shield/detector materials or broad windows, because SPI and COSI show activation/internal line backgrounds can dominate line analyses and COSI identifies 511 keV atmospheric plus activation contributions. Evidence: S12, S14, S17, S20, S24, S25.
- Old delayed dominance would not be proven by public literature alone; it still needs apples-to-apples local evidence on energy window, stage, material inventory, source normalization, and final selected events. Evidence for non-transferability and line/background modeling caveats: S13, S19, S21.

## Literature-Only Verdict

PASS_WITH_CAVEATS: The public literature supports a sanity-check conclusion that both outcomes are physically possible in different apertures and selections: delayed/activation dominance is expected for some Ge wide-field line instruments and material-rich configurations, while a pointed shielded TES-Laue narrow-window selection could plausibly have a much smaller final delayed component if local normalization and selection audits pass. Literature does not by itself identify a project BUG or NO_BUG; it only rules out direct absolute-rate transfer from X/XL-Calibur, SPI, or COSI. Evidence: S2, S4, S7, S9, S12, S14, S17, S19, S21, S24, S25.
